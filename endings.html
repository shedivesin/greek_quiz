These aren't random, and there's some regularity to it. I tried to find patterns in the table to make it easier to memorize, such as:

  • η is always feminine singular.

  • Genitive plural always ends in -ων.

  • The second declension genitive singular is -ου, and the neuter nominative/​accusative plural is -α. Other than those exceptions, accusative singular and genitive plural are always -ν, while genitive singular and accusative plural are always -ς.

  • Dative singular always ends in -ι. In particular, the subscript (-ῃ or -ῳ) is a dead giveaway.

  • Dative plural always ends in -ις or -σι.
